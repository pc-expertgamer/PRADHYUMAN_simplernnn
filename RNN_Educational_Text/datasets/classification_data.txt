An integer is a whole number that can be positive negative or zero not a fraction.|Math
Matrix multiplication involves a row-by-column product to yield a new matrix.|Math
A geometric series is a sum of terms where each term after the first is found by multiplying the previous one by a fixed non-zero number called the common ratio.|Math
Prime factorization expresses a number as a product of its prime number factors.|Math
A polynomial is an expression consisting of indeterminates and coefficients that involves only the operations of addition subtraction multiplication and non-negative integer exponentiation of variables.|Math
The Riemann hypothesis is a conjecture about the distribution of nontrivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function.|Math
In topology a manifold is a topological space that locally resembles Euclidean space near each point.|Math
A Fibonacci sequence is a series of numbers in which each number is the sum of the two preceding numbers.|Math
Set notation uses curly braces to list elements like A equals one two three.|Math
Logarithms are the inverse operation to exponentiation just as division is the inverse of multiplication.|Math
The Pythagorean identity states that sine squared theta plus cosine squared theta equals one for any angle theta.|Math
A vector is a quantity that has both magnitude and direction often represented by an arrow.|Math
The quadratic formula solves equations in the form ax squared plus bx plus c equals zero.|Math
Differential equations involve derivatives of an unknown function with respect to one or more variables.|Math
Eigenvalues and eigenvectors are special scalars and vectors associated with linear transformations.|Math
The binomial theorem provides a formula for expanding powers of binomials.|Math
A complex number has the form a plus bi where i is the imaginary unit defined as the square root of negative one.|Math
Group theory studies algebraic structures known as groups and their properties.|Math
The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus establishes the relationship between differentiation and integration.|Math
Bayes theorem describes the probability of an event based on prior knowledge of conditions related to the event.|Math

The Fourier transform decomposes a function of time into its constituent frequencies.|Math

An ecosystem includes all of the living things plants animals and organisms in a given area interacting with each other and also with their non-living environments weather earth sun soil climate atmosphere.|Science
The periodic table arranges chemical elements by atomic number electron configuration and recurring chemical properties.|Science
Natural selection is a core mechanism of evolution where organisms better adapted to their environment tend to survive and produce more offspring.|Science
A molecule is an electrically neutral group of two or more atoms held together by chemical bonds.|Science
Plate tectonics describes the large-scale motion of seven large plates and the movements of a larger number of smaller plates of Earth's lithosphere.|Science
The human brain controls thought memory emotion touch motor skills vision breathing temperature hunger and every process that regulates our body.|Science

Neurotransmitters are chemical messengers that transmit signals across a chemical synapse from one neuron to another target neuron.|Science
The electromagnetic spectrum encompasses all wavelengths of electromagnetic radiation from radio waves to gamma rays.|Science
Enzymes are biological molecules that significantly speed up the rate of virtually all chemical reactions that take place within cells.|Science

The theory of relativity describes the relationship between space and time and forms the basis for modern physics.|Science

Biodiversity refers to the variety and variability of life on Earth and is a measure of variation at the genetic species and ecosystem level.|Science
